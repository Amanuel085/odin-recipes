This is the recipe for a classic burger

How to make a burger

The classic burger is an all-time favorite that delivers juicy, flavorful beef patties, perfectly toasted buns, and simple yet delicious toppings. It’s the ultimate comfort food, customizable to suit your taste while staying true to its timeless appeal. With just a few quality ingredients, you can create a burger that rivals your favorite diner or backyard cookout.

IIn this recipe, we guide you step-by-step through crafting the perfect homemade burger—from shaping and seasoning the patties to assembling them with fresh toppings. Whether you like yours loaded with extras or kept simple, this burger recipe will help you achieve a juicy, well-balanced bite every time. Perfect for weeknight dinners, summer barbecues, or anytime you crave a satisfying handheld classic.

Ingidients

For the beef patties:

  • 1 pound (450g) ground beef (80/20 lean-to-fat ratio recommended)
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder (optional)
  • 1/2 teaspoon onion powder (optional)

For assembling:

  • 4 burger buns (sesame seed or plain)
  • 4 slices cheddar cheese (or your favorite cheese)
  • 4 leaves of crisp lettuce
  • 4 slices of ripe tomato
  • 4–8 slices of dill pickles
  • 1 small red onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 tablespoons mayonnaise
  • 2 tablespoons ketchup
  • 1 tablespoon mustard

Optional extras:

  • Crispy bacon strips
  • Avocado slices
  • Jalapeños for a spicy kick

Steps

  1. Prepare the patties
    • In a bowl, gently mix ground beef with salt, pepper, and optional seasonings (garlic or onion powder).
    • Divide into 4 equal portions and shape into patties, about 3/4-inch thick. Press a small indent in the center with your thumb (this prevents puffing up while cooking).
  2. Cook the patties
    • Heat a grill or skillet over medium-high heat. Lightly oil the surface.
    • Cook patties for 3–4 minutes on the first side until browned. Flip and cook for another 3–4 minutes, or until desired doneness.
    • Place a slice of cheese on each patty during the last minute of cooking and cover to melt.
  3. Toast the buns
    • Split the buns and lightly toast them on the grill or skillet until golden.
  4. Assemble the burgers
    • Spread mayonnaise on the bottom bun and ketchup/mustard on the top bun.
    • Layer lettuce, tomato, and pickles on the bottom bun.
    • Add the cheesy beef patty on top.
    • Finish with onion slices and any extra toppings you like.
    • Close with the top bun.
  5. Serve & enjoy
    • Serve immediately with fries, onion rings, or a simple side salad.
